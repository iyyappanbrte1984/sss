BIODIVERSITY & RED LIST

Understanding threatened species and cutting plastic in daily life

What is the IUCN Red List?

The IUCN Red List of Threatened Species is a global reference that shows how close different plants and animals are to extinction. Species are grouped into categories such as Least Concern, Vulnerable, Endangered and Critically Endangered based on scientific assessments.

Many freshwater and marine species are moving into higher‑risk categories because of pollution, plastic waste, habitat loss and climate change. Protecting lakes and rivers around you helps protect fish, amphibians, insects, birds and mammals along the entire river‑to‑ocean pathway.

Vulnerable (VU) Endangered (EN) Critically Endangered (CR) Extinct in the Wild (EW)

For detailed species lists and official information, always visit the IUCN Red List website and related resources directly.

Why Your Choices Matter

Every plastic item you refuse, reduce, reuse or recycle lowers the chance that it will reach lakes, rivers and the ocean. Less plastic in the water means cleaner habitats for fish, amphibians, birds, mammals and many other species.

By combining this self‑assessment with water‑quality measurements and ocean maps in your dashboard, students can see how daily habits connect to global goals under SDG 14: Life Below Water.

You can also explore UN and regional e‑learning courses on marine plastic pollution and circular economy to go deeper and become a local SDG champion.
